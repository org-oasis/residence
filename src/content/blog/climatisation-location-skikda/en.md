<h2>Air conditioning in Skikda rentals: the state of the offer</h2>

<p>Along the Skikda coast, from Stora to Filfila, air conditioning has become widespread in professional furnished residences built or renovated in recent years. The local Mediterranean climate, with hot summers and high coastal humidity, makes this amenity almost essential from June through the end of September.</p>

<h3>A now-standard amenity in Filfila</h3>

<p>Filfila concentrates the most recent and best-equipped residences in the wilaya. Air conditioning is installed there in the bedroom and living room almost systematically for professional rentals, unlike some older units in Skikda's city centre that may still rely on simple fans.</p>

<h3>What to check before booking</h3>

<p>Not all air conditioning units are equal. An old, poorly maintained or undersized unit for the room's surface area can prove disappointing during a full summer heatwave. A few signals help anticipate the actual quality of the equipment before booking.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Ask whether the air conditioning is reversible (heating and cooling) or cooling only</li>
  <li>Check that every main living room (bedroom, living room) has its own unit</li>
  <li>Ask about the date of the last servicing or filter cleaning</li>
  <li>Ask whether the noise level has been checked, an important point for sleep</li>
  <li>Confirm that air conditioning is included in the rate, with no consumption-based surcharge</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Reversible air conditioning</strong> offers a dual advantage on the Skikda coast: cooling rooms in summer and heating the unit during cooler months, with no need for a separate heating appliance.</p>

<h3>In winter: reversible heating mode</h3>

<h2>Best practices for use during the stay</h2>

<p>Sensible use of air conditioning improves comfort while limiting electricity consumption, a gesture appreciated in a residence where utilities are included in the rate.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Set the temperature around 24-26°C rather than the minimum, too large a gap with the outside being both uncomfortable and energy-intensive</li>
  <li>Keep doors and windows closed while the unit is running for optimal efficiency</li>
  <li>Avoid leaving the unit running continuously while away</li>
  <li>Slightly lower the temperature for young children, who are more sensitive to thermal shifts</li>
  <li>Report any unusual noise or drop in efficiency as soon as it appears rather than at the end of the stay</li>
</ul>

<h3>Air conditioning and young children</h3>

<p>For families travelling with young children, it's advisable to avoid too large a temperature gap with the outside and not to point the air flow directly at the bed or nap area. This point echoes the general precautions to take for a family stay on the Skikda coast.</p>
