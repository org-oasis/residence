<p>Filfila benefits from decent network coverage thanks to its proximity to Skikda's urban centre and its tourism development. <h3>What "high-speed Wi-Fi" concretely means</h3>

<p>The term "high-speed" covers very different realities depending on the listing. A connection genuinely suited to remote work or streaming needs to stay above 50 to 100 Mbps in a stable way, not only in occasional peaks. Below this threshold, video calls stutter and 4K streaming becomes difficult with several devices connected at once.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Under 20 Mbps: basic browsing only, limited HD streaming</li>
  <li>20 to 50 Mbps: comfortable HD streaming, one video call at a time</li>
  <li>50 to 100 Mbps: several devices, 4K streaming, decent remote work</li>
  <li>Over 100 Mbps: intensive professional use, several people working remotely at once</li>
</ul>

<h2>Wi-Fi and remote work from an apartment in Skikda</h2>

<p>The profile of the traveller combining a holiday with partial remote work is growing along the Algerian coast, particularly among diaspora members on an extended stay with family or in a second home. For this profile, Wi-Fi quality becomes a selection criterion on par with price or location.</p>

<h3>Best practices for a work-holiday stay</h3>

<ul>
  <li>Test the connection on arrival, before any important meeting scheduled the same day</li>
  <li>Favour a quiet space in the unit for video calls, such as the living room rather than a bedroom near the street</li>
  <li>Arrange a local mobile data plan as a backup for connection emergencies</li>
  <li>Check peak network hours, generally in the evening between 8pm and 11pm</li>
  <li>Ask in advance whether the chosen apartment has a dedicated desk or work space</li>
</ul>

<h3>Time difference and meeting slots</h3>

<p>Algeria does not observe seasonal daylight saving time, which simplifies scheduling meetings with contacts in Europe (a one-hour difference in summer, none with France in winter). This point makes coordination easier for remote workers staying in Skikda.</p>

<h2>What to do in case of a disconnection or slow speed</h2>

<p>Despite a reliable fiber infrastructure, an occasional network outage can still happen, generally linked to the local operator rather than the unit's internal setup. In that case, a quick message via WhatsApp allows a diagnosis or intervention within a short delay, a direct benefit of direct management by the residence rather than an intermediary platform.</p>

<h3>Useful steps in case of a slowdown</h3>

<ul>
  <li>Restart the router if access is available in the unit</li>
  <li>Check whether the slowdown affects the whole neighbourhood via a mobile data test</li>
  <li>Report the issue with the exact time to help diagnosis</li>
  <li>Temporarily switch to a mobile data hotspot for a professional emergency</li>
</ul>
