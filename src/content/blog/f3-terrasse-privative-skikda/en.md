<h2>Private terrace, balcony, loggia: know the difference</h2>

<p>Algerian real-estate vocabulary varies by advertiser, and the word "terrace" is particularly fuzzy. An <strong>apartment with private terrace</strong> looks nothing like a balcony or a loggia. Distinguishing the three avoids a bad surprise on arrival.</p>

<h3>Defining a real private terrace</h3>

<p>A private terrace is a flat outdoor space accessible only from the unit, large enough to host an outdoor dining set and to circulate without constraint. It is not crossed by a neighbour, not shared with several apartments, and has no maintenance easement requiring third-party passage.</p>

<h3>Classic pitfalls in Skikda listings</h3>

<p>Some listings use "terrace" to refer to a collective rooftop. Before confirming, ask for a plan or aerial photo proving the private nature of the access and the absence of direct overlook from immediate neighbours.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Rooftop terrace shared with several apartments</li>
  <li>Balcony under 4 m² presented as a terrace</li>
  <li>Half-glazed enclosed loggia with no direct opening</li>
  <li>Shared walkway or outdoor landing pitched as an amenity</li>
  <li>"Terrace" reachable via a common staircase or a through-room</li>
</ul>
