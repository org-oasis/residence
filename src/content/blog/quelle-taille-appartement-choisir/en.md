<p>The <strong>apartment size to choose based on the number of travellers</strong> comes down to a simple rule: a studio for 1 to 3 people, an F2 (classic or with jacuzzi) for 3 to 4 people, and an F3 with private terrace for 5 to 8 people once surcharges are included. Picking the wrong format is one of the most common mistakes when booking remotely: a studio booked for 4 people or an F3 rented for a couple alone create, on one hand real discomfort, on the other an unnecessary expense.</p>

<p>For a stay in Skikda, the capacity listed on each ad corresponds to comfortable sleeping arrangements, not a theoretical maximum crammed together. This guide details the real capacity of each format offered at Oasis Residence in Filfila, the rules for extra guests, and concrete recommendations depending on group composition: couple, family, group of friends or extended family.</p>

<h2>How apartment capacity is calculated in Skikda</h2>

<p>An <strong>apartment's capacity</strong> rests on two distinct notions that need to be kept separate before booking: base capacity, included in the advertised rate, and extra guests, charged on top.</p>

<h3>Base capacity included in the rate</h3>

<p>Each format shows a nightly rate calculated for a specific number of travellers. That number corresponds to real sleeping arrangements: double bed, single beds or a sofa bed, not an improvised extra mattress. Exceeding that number without flagging it at booking can create a bedding shortage on arrival.</p>

<h2>Deluxe Studio: the format for 1 to 3 travellers</h2>

<p>The <a href="/en/apartments/studio-deluxe-1">furnished studio for two</a> on the ground floor is the residence's most compact format. It suits a couple, a solo traveller or a couple with a young child perfectly. Its 23 m² includes a sleeping area, a living area and a fitted kitchen in a single open space.</p>

<h3>Why not to exceed 3 people in a studio</h3>

<p>Past 3 occupants, the studio's single-room layout becomes a limiting comfort factor, especially for a multi-night stay. Circulation between the kitchen corner, the living area and the sleeping area tightens quickly once luggage and children's equipment (stroller, travel cot) are added. The studio remains an excellent budget choice for a couple or a solo traveller seeking immediate proximity to the sea without needing extra space.</p>

<h2>Classic and Modern F2: the format for a family of 4</h2>

<p>The <a href="/en/apartments/f2-classique-2">F2 family-friendly apartment</a> is the residence's most versatile format. With a closed bedroom and a separate living room that can host an extra bed, it matches the most common traveller profile booking in Skikda: two adults and two children, or a couple with a teenager and a young child.</p>

<h3>Closed bedroom vs open living room</h3>

<p>The separation between the bedroom and the living room is the F2's main advantage over the studio: parents get a closed space for the night while children sleep in the living room, which helps with different sleep schedules, especially with a baby or a young child.</p>

<h3>F2 with jacuzzi: same capacity, different use</h3>

<p>The <a href="/en/apartments/f2-jacuzzi-3">F2 with private jacuzzi</a> shares the same base capacity (4 people) as the classic F2, but its typical use differs: it targets a couple or a small family seeking a premium stay rather than a larger family primarily looking for space. The jacuzzi basin requires constant parental supervision with young children, making it a less suitable format than a classic F2 for a family with several small children.</p>

<h2>F3 with private terrace: the format for 5 to 8 travellers</h2>

<p>The <a href="/en/apartments/f3-terrasse-7">F3 with private terrace</a> is sized for larger groups: an extended family with grandparents, two smaller families travelling together, or a group of friends. Its base capacity of 6 people can rise to 8 with surcharges for extra guests.</p>

<h3>Three bedrooms and a terrace to relieve the shared space</h3>

<p>With three distinct rooms and a private outdoor terrace, the F3 avoids the pitfall of a group crammed into a single living room. The terrace serves as an extra living space for meals or evenings, easing pressure indoors during the hottest hours of the day.</p>

<h3>Why an F3 is often cheaper than combining small apartments</h3>

<p>For a group of 6 to 8, comparing the rate of a single F3 to that of two separate F2 units often tips the balance toward the F3: a single rent, a single set of utilities (water, electricity, Wi-Fi), and above all a group kept together for meals and beach trips instead of coordinating two separate units.</p>

<h2>Special cases to anticipate</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Baby under 2 years old</strong>: generally not counted in base capacity, but a baby cot should be requested at booking regardless of the chosen format.</li>
  <li><strong>Teenagers</strong>: count as adults in the capacity calculation; a couple with two teenagers already exceeds studio capacity and naturally moves toward an F2.</li>
  <li><strong>Mixed group of singles</strong>: prefer an F3 to preserve each person's privacy thanks to the three separate rooms, rather than an F2 shared by four strangers in one living room.</li>
  <li><strong>Family with grandparents</strong>: the F3 with terrace offers a more flexible pace, useful when rest needs differ between generations.</li>
  <li><strong>Long stay (7 nights or more)</strong>: sizing the format correctly from the start avoids switching apartments mid-stay, rarely possible depending on availability.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Common sizing mistakes to avoid</h2>

<ul>
  <li>Booking a studio for 4 people to save money, at the cost of degraded comfort over several nights</li>
  <li>Forgetting to flag extra guests, which complicates preparing extra bedding on arrival</li>
  <li>Underestimating the space needed for holiday luggage (suitcases, beach gear, stroller)</li>
  <li>Booking an F3 for a couple alone out of caution, when an F2 with jacuzzi offers a better value for that profile</li>
  <li>Neglecting room allocation for a mixed group, a source of avoidable tension with a better-planned booking</li>
</ul>
