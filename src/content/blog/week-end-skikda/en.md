<p>A <strong>weekend in Skikda</strong> is built around two complementary axes: the Filfila beaches, a few minutes from the residence, and Stora harbour, further west, which concentrates the sea excursions and a different, more maritime and lively atmosphere. In two days, it is possible to combine lounging, a water activity and a real change of scenery, without the long journeys that often weigh down short breaks.</p>

<p>For a traveller pressed for time, a couple or a family visiting from Algiers, Constantine, Annaba or abroad, the question is not whether there is enough to do in Skikda for a weekend, but rather how to split the available time between beach, discovery and rest. This article sets out a concrete day-by-day programme, options depending on the type of trip (family, couple, friends), the right moves for getting around, and a realistic budget for two nights on site.</p>

<h2>A weekend in Skikda: how much time for what</h2>

<p>Two days and two nights are enough to make the most of the area without feeling rushed. The key is not to try to see everything: it is better to devote a full half-day to each main activity (beach, boat excursion, short hike) rather than rushing through sites at speed.</p>

<h3>The recommended pace for a short stay</h3>

<p>Arriving on Friday evening or early Saturday morning allows two full days to be enjoyed. Leaving on Sunday in the late afternoon leaves time for one last swim before the road. This pace suits a couple as much as a family with children, provided activity and rest are alternated rather than travel piled up.</p>

<h2>Which time of year to plan a weekend in Skikda</h2>

<p>The bathing season runs from June to November, leaving a wide window for planning a weekend. July and August offer the warmest sea but also the heaviest crowds and the highest rates, with booking best done several weeks ahead to be sure of a spot. A weekend in June or September remains just as pleasant weather-wise, with fewer people on the beaches and noticeably more accessible rates. Off-season, from October to May, a weekend takes on a different character: less focused on extended swimming, but still enjoyable for coastal walks, local food and quiet, with some of the lowest accommodation rates of the year.</p>

<h3>Practical landmarks around Filfila</h3>

<p>Filfila is also home to hotel complexes known in the region, such as the 4-star Rusica Park and the 5-star Royal Tulip, which often serve as geographic landmarks in the area. Without necessarily being a stop on the itinerary, these establishments mark useful reference points for locating beaches, restaurants and sea access relative to the chosen accommodation.</p>

<h2>Day 1: Filfila, beaches and relaxation</h2>

<p>The first day deliberately focuses on Filfila and its immediate surroundings, to enjoy the sea without losing time travelling right from arrival.</p>

<h3>Morning: Larbi Ben M'hidi beach</h3>

<p>Larbi Ben M'hidi beach (formerly Jeanne d'Arc) remains the area's reference point: fine sand, easy access and immediate proximity to Filfila's serviced residences. It is the ideal spot for a quiet morning swim, before crowds build up around midday in high season.</p>

<h3>Afternoon: lounging or Marina d'Or Water Park</h3>

<p>For a family with children, the afternoon can shift to the <strong>Marina d'Or Water Park</strong> in Filfila, a water park that breaks up the classic beach routine and keeps younger visitors occupied for several hours. For a couple or a group without children, continuing to relax on the beach or exploring the neighbouring Grande Plage remains a simple alternative.</p>

<h3>Evening: restaurant by the sea</h3>

<p>The first evening lends itself well to a meal of fresh fish or local specialities at one of the area's establishments, before a night in an apartment a few minutes' walk from the sea.</p>

<h2>Day 2: Stora, boat excursion and Grande Plage</h2>

<p>The second day heads towards Stora, west of Skikda, for a change of atmosphere and a different activity from the classic beach day.</p>

<h3>Morning: Stora harbour and a sea excursion</h3>

<p>Stora harbour is the most practical starting point for a <strong>boat excursion</strong> along the rocky coastline, with its coves and sea caves that are hard to reach otherwise. It is the flagship activity for breaking out of a beach-only programme and seeing the coast from another angle.</p>

<h3>Afternoon: Grande Plage and Les Calatines</h3>

<p>The afternoon can be split between Grande Plage, larger and livelier, and Les Calatines, more low-key, for one last swim before heading back. Those who prefer walking can follow up with the short Stora-Carrière hike, doable in around thirty minutes.</p>

<h2>Where to stay for a successful weekend</h2>

<p>Filfila remains the most practical location for a short weekend: the residence is 700 m from the sea, letting you reach the beach on foot and save the car for trips to Stora or an excursion. <h2>Getting around during the weekend</h2>

<p>Without a car, the Filfila beaches remain reachable by taxi from Skikda town centre, but Stora and a boat excursion require more planning. Renting a car for two days noticeably simplifies travel and allows several beaches to be combined in the same day without relying on taxi schedules.</p>

<h3>From Algiers, Constantine or Annaba</h3>

<p>For visitors coming from another wilaya, the weekend often starts with a road or air trip to Skikda. Those arriving by car from Algiers or Constantine gain flexibility once on site, while travellers arriving by plane or bus tend to opt for a local car rental or taxis, largely sufficient for a programme centred on Filfila and Stora.</p>

<h2>What to do if the weather isn't ideal</h2>

<p>An overcast sky or stronger wind doesn't rule out enjoying the weekend. Coastal walking remains practical in cool weather, and fish restaurants or seafront cafés offer a pleasant alternative during passing rain. A boat excursion, on the other hand, depends directly on sea conditions: it's better to plan a flexible slot rather than a fixed time if the weather is uncertain at the time of booking.</p>
